It’s Pumpkin season. Whether you are buying everything pumpkin or buying pumpkins and making your own creations, tis’ the season to make your pumpkin loving self happy. It is Riddle Wednesday, so here are some riddles about pumpkins.

I start as a seed and grow on a vine,
In October, I’m ripe, and you’ll find me in line.
What am I?

I’m the star of a pie, so creamy and sweet,
In the autumn months, I’m a real treat.
What am I?

I’m a field of orange, as far as you can see,
Ready to be picked and taken home with glee.
What am I?

I’m a patch full of fun, a place to explore,
With hayrides and mazes, and pumpkins galore.
What am I?

I’m full of seeds, so many to count,
Roasted and salted, I make a tasty snack, no doubt.
What am I?

I’m used as a symbol, for a holiday so grand,
A carriage for Cinderella, in a magical land.
What am I?

I’m a part of a contest, a fun fall tradition,
Guess my weight and win, it’s a pumpkin mission.
What am I?

I’m a spice in your latte, a flavor so warm,
With cinnamon and nutmeg, I’ll keep you from harm.
What am I?

I’m used as a target, for a game of skill,
Tossing rings or darts, aim for the thrill.
What am I?

I’m a fruit with a name that sounds quite quaint,
In pies and lattes, I’m a fall favorite, ain’t I quaint?
What am I?

I’m the main ingredient in a seasonal soup,
With creamy goodness, I’ll make your taste buds whoop.
What am I?

I’m a pumpkin patch visitor’s ultimate quest,
Find me, take me home, and put me to the test.
What am I?
